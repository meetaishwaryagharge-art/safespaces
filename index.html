How It Works

Effective Therapy Process

The process of effective therapy begins with building a strong rapport between the therapist and the client, creating a safe and trusting environment.

01

Understanding Emotional Changes

As children, we express our feelings freely. But as we grow, we begin to hide emotions due to fear of judgment and the need to fit in.

02

Creating a Safe Space

A safe and welcoming space where you can share openly, feel heard, and receive support without judgment.

03

Exploring & Rediscovering Yourself

Helping you explore emotions, gain clarity, and reconnect with your inner strength and identity.

04

Taking the First Step Toward Healing

Healing begins with one step—giving yourself time, compassion, and a safe space to grow.

Build Client Rapport

In the beginning of therapy, the therapist focuses on helping you feel comfortable and safe in the environment, building a genuine connection.

Understand Patterns

Once comfortable, the therapist wants to know more about you, your childhood, your past, your family and the thoughts you might be feeling.

Provide Perspective

The therapist will psycho-educate the explanations behind your patterns so that you can be consciously aware of them and their reasons.

Set Goals Together

Your therapist will help you identify unhealthy mechanisms and orient you to set a goal toward healthy mental health, tracking progress along the way.

Equip You with Strategies

Once you know what you are willing to work for, the therapist will help equip you with tools and strategies to reach your goal sooner.

Help Them Recognize Little Wins

Your therapist will also be your cheerleader to help you acknowledge and celebrate the little victories you achieve during the process.

Work Ethics

The principles that guide every interaction, every session, and every decision we make.

Beneficence and Non-Maleficence

I strive to protect the rights and welfare of those with whom I share professional relations. I aim to offer services which are free of biases, affiliations, and prejudices that might influence the quality of my work.

Fidelity and Responsibility

I seek to cultivate a profession and a scientific community built on trust, accountability, and ethical considerations for all individuals. I recognize my moral responsibility towards the community and aim to work towards the betterment of the community.

Integrity

I do not keep the intentions to deceive or misrepresent during the conduct of my services. I strive to provide transparency and honesty in my practice.

Justice

I hold the responsibility to be fair and impartial. I am aware of my areas of competence and limitations. I aim to practice within the areas of my expertise. I strive to treat all my clients equally and impartially.

Respect for People's Rights and Dignity

I respect the right to privacy, dignity, and confidentiality of all the individuals I am professionally associated with. I may sometimes break confidentiality under exceptional circumstances when there is presence of threat to client or others.

Professional Excellence

I commit to ongoing learning, self-reflection, and professional development to ensure that the care I provide remains current, effective, and grounded in evidence-based practice.

Professional Evaluation

Psychological Assessments

Browsing the internet can sometimes lead us to self-diagnose our mental health concerns. But how accurate are these diagnoses? Most of the time, they are not.

Understanding your personality and mental well-being requires standardized, reliable, and scientifically validated psychological assessments. These assessments help provide an accurate and comprehensive evaluation of your concerns.

The process begins with an intake session, where we take the time to understand your experiences and concerns in depth. An early and accurate assessment helps in identifying the root of the concern, allowing for timely and effective support and treatment.

Types of Assessments

Assessment Types

Personality Assessments

Helps understand personality traits, behavior patterns, and emotional responses. Provides insights for personal growth, relationships, and therapy planning.

Specific Learning Disability Assessments

Identifies learning difficulties in reading, writing, spelling, or mathematics. Helps detect conditions like dyslexia and provides support for academic improvement.

Couples and Family Assessments

Evaluates relationship dynamics, communication patterns, and conflicts. Helps couples and families build healthier and stronger relationships.

Intellectual Assessments

Measures cognitive abilities like reasoning, memory, and problem-solving. Helps understand intellectual strengths and learning capacities.

Developmental Assessments

Evaluates a child's developmental milestones and overall growth. Helps identify developmental delays and ensures early support.

Corporate Assessments

Psychological assessments for corporate help evaluate employee behavior, personality, and cognitive abilities. They support better hiring decisions and workplace productivity.

Psychotherapy & Counselling

We offer psychotherapy and counselling to support individuals facing a variety of emotional and mental health concerns.

Therapeutic Interventions

Therapeutic interventions are techniques used by mental health professionals to help individuals manage emotional, psychological, or behavioral difficulties.

Supportive Therapy

A therapeutic approach that provides emotional support, reassurance, and guidance to help individuals cope with stress and life challenges. It focuses on strengthening a person's existing coping abilities.

Cognitive Behaviour Therapy (CBT)

A structured therapy that helps individuals identify and change negative thoughts and behaviors. It focuses on developing healthier thinking patterns and coping strategies to improve emotional well-being.

Rational Emotive Behaviour Therapy (REBT)

A form of therapy that helps people identify irrational beliefs and replace them with more rational, positive thoughts. It aims to improve emotional well-being and behavior.

Dialectical Behaviour Therapy (DBT)

A therapy that helps individuals manage intense emotions and improve relationships. It focuses on mindfulness, emotional regulation, distress tolerance, and interpersonal skills.

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

ACT encourages individuals to accept their thoughts and feelings instead of fighting them. It focuses on mindfulness and committing to actions that align with personal values.

Transactional Analysis

A psychological approach that studies interactions between people to understand communication and behavior patterns. It helps individuals improve relationships and self-awareness.

Trauma-Informed Care

An approach that recognizes the impact of trauma on a person's life and mental health. It focuses on creating a safe, supportive environment that promotes healing and recovery.

Positive Psychology

A field of psychology that focuses on strengths, happiness, and well-being. It encourages individuals to build positive emotions, resilience, and meaningful life experiences.

Common Questions

FAQs

1. What challenges are you currently facing in your personal or emotional life?
Many individuals face challenges such as stress, anxiety, relationship issues, work pressure, or feelings of loneliness. Recognizing these challenges is the first step toward seeking appropriate support.
2. How do you usually cope with stress or difficult emotions?
People cope through activities like talking to someone they trust, practicing mindfulness, exercising, or seeking professional support. Therapy can help you develop healthier and more effective coping strategies.
3. What motivated you to seek therapy or support at Safe Spaces?
The desire to improve mental well-being, find guidance, and have a safe environment to express thoughts and emotions motivates individuals to seek therapy.
4. Do you feel comfortable talking about your thoughts and feelings with others?
Some people feel comfortable, while others find it difficult, which is why therapy provides a confidential and supportive space. We create an environment where you can gradually open up at your own pace.
5. What kind of support are you hoping to receive from therapy?
Individuals often look for emotional support, coping strategies, guidance for personal growth, and help in managing stress or anxiety. Our therapist tailors each session to your specific needs and goals.
6. What changes would you like to see in your mental and emotional well-being?
People often hope to feel more confident, emotionally balanced, less stressed, and better able to handle life's challenges. Setting clear goals helps make therapy more focused and effective.

Questions Answered

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the difference between a clinical psychologist and a therapist?
A therapist or counsellor mainly provides counselling for emotional concerns like stress, relationships, or grief. A clinical psychologist has advanced training and can conduct psychological assessments, diagnose mental health conditions, and provide evidence-based treatment.
2. Who should consult a clinical psychologist?
Anyone experiencing anxiety, depression, stress, trauma, or relationship difficulties can consult a clinical psychologist for professional support. You don't have to wait until things feel unbearable — early support is always beneficial.
3. How can therapy help?
Therapy helps you understand your thoughts, emotions, and behaviors while developing healthier coping strategies for better well-being. It provides a safe space to explore challenges and work toward meaningful personal growth.
4. Are therapy sessions confidential?
Yes, therapy sessions are completely confidential and provide a safe, non-judgmental space to share your concerns. Confidentiality may only be broken in exceptional circumstances where there is a serious risk of harm.
